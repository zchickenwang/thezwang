Random Notes

27 March 2018

Morning in Paris

Yawning eyelids peeled open a world of pale white. Piercing warmth of a familiar sight not yet recognized. Or perhaps it was the winter sun that chilled the morning air but soothed her sleeping children. Limbs still too heavy to compel, I lay motionless watching the ceiling age.

The brilliancy of rebirth dissipates with dream-like urgency; it subsists only in unreliable recollections and as a fading footprint of a glow on the back of the eyes. Quickly I shut mine in protest but alas, broken locks cannot be fixed, and undeniable curiosity pries apart the floodgates once more&emdash;wholly drained of her milky luster, wholly naked to life's stripping gaze, the exposed enamel epidermis shrivels before my eyes. Bloodless veins tear cracks through retreating skin on which sickly bumps and blotches sprout and seep like coffee soaking parchment; I cry out to save the pure, yet each blink delivers another shattering blow to the vision before me. So in this self-induced irony (which had suitably replaced the blanket now thrown aside) I basked, observing the dying convulsions of my subconscious mind with the dry, guiltless wonder of a child roasting an ant&emdash;no, it was so much more swift, unnatural&emdash;rather, like witnessing execution by guillotine, in a time lapse.

These white walls alone know how long I lay in the morning light tightly bound in introspection, twisting and turning in search of the silence to hear their moribund whispers and grasp quelque chose de profond beneath their daily crucifixion. Ce que fait le fossoyeur devient riant, fait par l'enfant&emdash;I tug at the words like string through a steak. The work of death then, perhaps is felt only but upon infancy of the day . . . I roll over once more but it's too late&emdash;rest flees the mind that houses the dead, and indeed I felt a newfound vitality gushing forth from the fresh memory of slaughter. Wholly content to start the day, I sat up.
